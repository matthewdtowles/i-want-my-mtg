6.1 Spike: Buylist Data Sources & Price-Data Architecture

Status: recommendation  ·  Date: June 2026  ·  Epic: Phase 6 (#498)  ·  Cross-repo: scry#14, iwantmymtg-mcp#9

Deliverable for ROADMAP 6.1. Decides price-data sourcing and the storage approach before the 6.2-6.5 build.

1. The question

Phase 6 wants to show buylist (sell-to-vendor) prices next to retail, highlight the best buylist offer per card, and help users decide who to sell to in bulk. Today we only store an averaged retail price, so before building we need to know: where does buylist data come from, how often, how hard is it to pull, what does it cost, how many vendors does it cover, and what are the terms-of-service and maintenance costs.

2. What we have today

  • scry pulls MTGJSON's retail path and averages across providers (TCGplayer, Card Kingdom, Cardsphere) into a single normal/foil value.
  • The web price table stores that one averaged value per card per day, keyed by the MTGJSON card uuid. Cards also carry tcgplayer_product_id.
  • The averaging is what blocks per-vendor comparison, not a shortage of data. Buylist is dropped entirely.
Matching is mostly already solved. Our card.id is the MTGJSON uuid, which is the same key MTGJSON prices use. MTGJSON also exposes scryfallId and the TCGplayer product IDs, so scry can carry whatever join key a second source needs at ingest time rather than reconstructing it later.

Notes on the realistic options

MTGJSON (what scry already pulls). Daily, free, and the AllPrices file carries the last 90 days, so we get history for free. Retail comes from TCGplayer, Cardmarket, Card Kingdom, Cardsphere, and now Mana Pool (a newer addition). Buylist comes from only Card Kingdom and Cardsphere, and Cardsphere is really an aggregate of member credit offers rather than a store you sell to. One known caveat: MTGJSON's Card Kingdom buylist can show a buy price even when CK is buying zero copies (MTGJSON issue #1142), so its buylist is indicative, not "is this offer real right now."

Card Kingdom direct pricelist. A public, no-auth JSON dump at api.cardkingdom.com/api/v2/pricelist (plus a sealed equivalent). It returned more than 10MB in testing, which confirms it is the whole catalog in one file. It includes retail and buylist, the number of copies CK is currently buying, the condition, and a scryfall_id for matching. That live buy quantity is exactly what the inventory and optimizer features (6.4, 6.5) need, and it fixes the MTGJSON qty=0 problem. Free, daily, one vendor, but it is the anchor US buylist vendor.

Scryfall is free and daily but retail only, so it does not move us toward buylist. Useful only as a retail cross-check or image source (which we already use it for).

TCGCSV is a free daily mirror of TCGplayer market prices keyed by the TCGplayer product ID we already store. Retail only. It would let us hold a true per-card TCGplayer market price instead of the MTGJSON average, but it re-publishes TCGplayer data, so there is some terms-of-service gray area.

TCGplayer official API is closed to new applicants since the eBay acquisition; existing keys are grandfathered. This closure is why the whole third-party ecosystem exists. Treat as unavailable.

Paid aggregators (JustTCG, TCGAPIs) are easy to use and TCGAPIs spans many marketplaces on one product ID, but the "buylist" they expose is mostly marketplace lowest-sell or store-credit numbers rather than a clean multi-vendor cash buylist, and TCGAPIs runs £99-£499/mo. Hard to justify before the feature proves itself.

go-mtgban is the open-source Go library behind the community buylist-comparison tools, with scrapers for many vendors' retail and buylist (Card Kingdom, Star City Games, ABU Games, and more). It is the only realistic path to broad "who pays most across 5+ stores" coverage, but the cost is running and maintaining scrapers and carrying the terms-of-service risk per vendor. The hosted MTGBAN service is paid/invite.

4. Recommendation

A phased, additive approach that fits the epic's direction (separate granular store, derive today's aggregates from it). The headline: we do not need many APIs to start. MTGJSON plus one free Card Kingdom endpoint covers the entire first cut of Phase 6.

Tier A - nowExpand scry to capture the full MTGJSON tree

Free, no new API. Stop averaging at ingest. Store every provider's retail plus Card Kingdom and Cardsphere buylist, per finish, per day, in the new granular store, and derive today's averaged normal/foil from it so nothing downstream breaks. This alone delivers buylist for two vendors at zero new cost and zero new integration.

Tier B - nextAdd Card Kingdom's direct pricelist

Free, one integration. Upgrades CK buylist from indicative to actionable by adding live buy quantity and condition, which 6.4 and 6.5 actually need. Card Kingdom is the anchor US buylist vendor.

Tier C - laterBroad multi-vendor buylist, only if demand shows

Defer. Once Tier A/B prove users want "best across many stores," reach for either the go-mtgban scrapers (free, broad, but you own maintenance and ToS risk) or a paid aggregator (TCGAPIs, ~£99-£499/mo). Do not take on scraper maintenance or a monthly bill speculatively.

Recommended anchor: Tier A + Tier B. Both are free, both are low-maintenance, and together they give actionable Card Kingdom buylist plus indicative Cardsphere buylist with full per-provider retail. Tier C stays a documented option, not a commitment.

5. Design implications (spike bullets 2-4)

5.1 Granular price store (capture all, derive aggregates)

Key each row on the card uuid plus the dimensions that make a price distinct:

granular_price (                         -- current per-vendor offer, one row per series
  card_id      varchar                    -- MTGJSON uuid, FK to card.id
  provider     varchar                    -- tcgplayer | cardkingdom | cardsphere | manapool
  price_type   varchar                    -- 'retail' | 'buylist'
  finish       varchar                    -- 'normal' | 'foil' | 'etched'
  condition    varchar NOT NULL DEFAULT 'NM'  -- 'NM' by convention; MTGJSON has no grade
  date         date                       -- as-of date of the current offer (not in the key)
  price        numeric NOT NULL
  PRIMARY KEY (card_id, provider, price_type, finish, condition)
)
-- granular_price_history: same columns, retention-bounded; PK adds date
--   PRIMARY KEY (card_id, provider, price_type, finish, condition, date)
  • Derive the existing average from this store: today's normal = average of retail across providers for the normal finish (and likewise foil), matching current behavior exactly so no downstream feature changes.
  • Additive rollout. Stand it up alongside the current price ingestion so existing pricing keeps working unchanged while the granular path is validated, then switch the derived value to read from it.
  • History/retention mirrors the existing policy (daily for 7 days, weekly to 28 days, monthly beyond), applied per (card, provider, type, finish, condition) series.

5.2 Vendor model (for grouping and the optimizer)

Separate the data source from the sellable vendor. A provider is where a number came from; a vendor is somewhere a user can actually sell, and carries the rules the optimizer needs:

  • store-credit bonus % (and whether the bonus applies to credit vs cash)
  • accepted conditions and finish handling
  • minimum / maximum order constraints

Today only Card Kingdom and Cardsphere qualify as real sell-to vendors, which keeps the 6.5 optimizer small at first. Star City Games, ABU, and others arrive only with Tier C.

5.3 Matching second sources

  • MTGJSON is the same uuid we already key on (free).
  • Card Kingdom direct joins via scryfall_id or set + collector number, both of which scry already sees in MTGJSON, so the mapping is carried at ingest, not reconstructed later.

6.2 Granular store Build the granular_price table above, web ingestion fed by scry's expanded output, derive the current averaged value from it, and apply the existing retention tiers.
6.3 Card views Show buylist price(s) per card, highlight the best offer and which vendor, respect normal/foil, and fit the existing price section / overlay patterns.
6.4 Inventory tools Select a subset (or all) of inventory, compute best buylist per item, total it, group by vendor (what each vendor would pay across the selection), CSV export. Card Kingdom's live buy quantity makes this actionable.
6.5 Optimizer Sell-list / buy-list entities per user; optimizer favors consolidating into a single vendor and factors store-credit bonus %. Starts with CK + Cardsphere; mirror via iwantmymtg-mcp#9.

7. Open questions

  • Do we want true per-card TCGplayer market price (via TCGCSV) instead of the MTGJSON average for retail, given the TCGCSV terms-of-service gray area? Default for now: keep MTGJSON-derived retail.
  • Condition handling: MTGJSON has no condition; CK-direct does. Do we surface CK's NM-only buylist as-is, or normalize to a single per-card buylist? Default: store condition where present, display NM by default.
  • When (if ever) Tier C is justified, do we prefer maintaining go-mtgban scrapers or paying for an aggregator? Defer the decision until 6.3/6.4 show demand for breadth.
